The Theory: Behavioral Biometrics via Formal Languages

Modern transparent anti-bot systems typically rely on heavy statistical machine learning models to analyze typing behavior. However, this engine implements a deterministic mathematical approach based directly on Theory of Computation: it treats keystroke rhythms as a formal string belonging to a defined language, utilizing Finite Automata graph theory to classify the "author" as human or machine.

1. The Keystroke Capture Engine
Event Trigger: User presses a key on the keyboard.
Measurement: The system calculates the Inter-Keystroke Interval (IKI) — the exact milliseconds elapsed since the previous keypress:
IKIn = Timestampn - Timestampn-1
2. Lexical Analysis (Symbol Encoding)

The raw millisecond intervals are instantly translated into discrete symbols to construct a formal sequence over our vocabulary alphabet Σ = {F, M, S, R, P, N}:

F (Fast) IKI < 120ms.
Common in automated scripts or reflexive bursts.
M (Medium) 120ms - 300ms.
The standard human typing speed range.
S (Slow) 300ms - 700ms.
Deliberate, methodical, or hunt-and-peck typing.
P (Pause) IKI > 700ms.
Uniquely human "think-pauses".
R (Repetitive) 4+ identical timings in a row. A hallmark of a macro loop.
N (Normal) Catch-all dynamic baseline for unclassified timings.
3. Classification via Finite Automata Graphs
Bot Detection (Deterministic)

Machines type with mathematical perfection. We run the stream through rigid Deterministic Finite Automata (DFA) that immediately "Accept" (flag as a bot) if they hit constraints like exactly 5 'F' symbols in a row without deviation, or an unbroken loop of 'R' symbols representing a programmatic macro loop.

Human Detection (Nondeterministic)

Humans are erratic by nature! Our Human Automaton relies heavily on detecting Variety and crucially, Pauses ('P'). We also map out a Nondeterministic Finite Automaton (NFA) with ε-transitions (moves without consuming a symbol) to elegantly model the probabilistic "choppiness" and uncertainty inherent in genuine human behavior.

4. Final Scoring Analytics
Beyond purely testing binary state graph acceptance, the final verdict considers the overarching mathematical distribution of your timing properties:

• Coefficient of Variation (CV): The standard deviation divided by the mean. Bots have a CV near 0 (perfect uniformity). Humans naturally feature a high variance coefficient.
• Pause Logic: Scripts fundamentally do not pause to "think" about what character comes next.
• Max Run Length: The longest consecutive streak of identical categorical timings. Extended identical strings indicate artificial latency injection instead of biological motor processes.
